You've been accepted, you've got a start date, and now you need somewhere to live. The University of Auckland's halls fill up fast — and they cost what they cost. The private flatting market is the alternative most students end up in by second year, and the difference between a good year and a rough one usually comes down to the suburb, the commute, and what you signed without reading.

Here's the practical picture: where students actually live, what rooms cost, and what to check before you commit to a place near the University of Auckland.

---

## The two campuses, and why it matters where you live

Most of the University is on the **City Campus** — the block bounded by Symonds Street, Princes Street, Wellesley Street East, and Alfred Street. That's where Arts, Business, Science, Engineering, Law, and the Library all sit. If you're studying any of those, "near uni" means within walking distance of Symonds Street or a short bus ride to it.

The other campus that matters for student housing is **Grafton** — 85 Park Road, next to Auckland City Hospital. That's the Faculty of Medical and Health Sciences and the School of Pharmacy. If you're a med, nursing, pharmacy, or optometry student, your campus is in Grafton — not the City Campus — and your commute and your evening study patterns will look different.

The two campuses are about a 15-minute walk apart, so a flat that's good for one is usually fine for the other. But if you've got 8am clinical placements at the hospital, knowing this matters more than it sounds.

## Halls of residence: what they actually cost

For first-years, halls are the path of least resistance. You're surrounded by other first-years, meals are sorted (in catered options), and you don't have to negotiate a tenancy agreement before you've even started classes.

The trade-off is cost and competition. University of Auckland halls were 99.8% full at the start of 2025, and the University has been working to expand capacity. Apply the moment your offer is confirmed.

The 2026 hall picture, in rough numbers (always confirm against the [University Accommodation page](https://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/on-campus/accommodation.html) before you budget):

| Type | Examples | Approx weekly cost (2026) |
|---|---|---|
| Catered (meals included) | Waipārūrū, Te Tirohanga o te Tōangaroa, O'Rorke, University Hall Towers | $475–$625 |
| Self-catered apartment-style | Carlaw Park, Whitaker | $370–$465 |
| Studio / single-room (self-catered) | Apartments at Symonds (and similar) | $400–$520 |

Catered halls look expensive on the sticker, but you're paying for accommodation, food, internet, power, and a structured first-year environment. Compare like with like — a self-catered flat at $310/week plus $80/week of groceries plus $40/week of power and internet is closer to $430/week, before you've considered the time cost of cooking and shopping.

> **Key point:** Halls are priced by semester or year, not weekly, and most have a 38-week or 44-week residential period. If you're staying in Auckland over summer, you'll need a separate plan — halls usually close or charge a different summer rate. Plan that gap before you sign.

The University also has a **HomeStay** programme matching students with Auckland host families. It's typically used by international and younger first-year students who want full board with a family rather than a hall environment.

## Where students flat: suburbs by commute

By second year, most students are in the private market. The decision is mostly a trade-off between rent and commute time. Here's the practical map for the City Campus and Grafton:

### Walking distance (10–20 minutes)

| Suburb | Approx room rent (shared flat) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Grafton | $280–$340/week | Closest to the hospital and med school; mostly apartments. The University's Grafton Student Flats are no longer accepting new tenancies, so the private market is more competitive than it used to be. |
| Eden Terrace | $280–$340/week | Walkable to Symonds St; mix of older flats and newer apartments. |
| Newton | $270–$330/week | Smaller, slightly cheaper than Eden Terrace; sits on the K Road ridge. |
| City Centre / CBD | $290–$380/week | Apartment-only; some buildings are leasehold and some have weathertight history — ask. |

### Bus belt (15–25 minutes)

| Suburb | Approx room rent (shared flat) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Kingsland | $260–$320/week | Frequent Inner Link and Western buses; trains from Kingsland Station. |
| Mt Eden | $270–$330/week | Mostly older villas converted into flats; some great character, some genuinely cold. |
| Sandringham | $240–$290/week | Bus or short Western Line connection via Mt Albert. Cheaper rooms, more space. |
| Parnell | $310–$370/week | Walkable to City Campus across the Domain; pricier and quieter. |

### Western Line train (25–45 minutes)

| Suburb | Approx room rent (shared flat) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Mt Albert | $240–$290/week | Direct Western Line train to Maungawhau (Mt Eden) and Britomart. |
| Avondale | $220–$270/week | Cheaper rooms, larger sections; train roughly every 10–15 minutes peak. |
| New Lynn | $220–$270/week | Same line; slightly further but better town centre. |
| Henderson | $210–$260/week | Cheapest of the Western Line suburbs commonly used by students. |

### Eastern and Southern Line options

| Suburb | Approx room rent | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Glen Innes | $210–$260/week | Eastern Line; 25–30 minutes to Britomart. |
| Newmarket | $290–$360/week | One stop from Maungawhau on the Southern Line; walkable to Grafton in 15–20 minutes. |

Room rents are indicative — what you actually pay depends on the property, number of flatmates, and what's included (some flats include power and internet, most don't). For whole-property benchmarks in any specific suburb, the [Tenancy Services Market Rent tool](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/rent-bond-and-bills/market-rent/) is the most reliable source.

The new **City Rail Link** opens through 2026, with the Maungawhau (Mt Eden) interchange dramatically reducing travel time from Western Line suburbs into the central city. If you're choosing between Eden Terrace at $310/week and Mt Albert at $260/week, the train math has gotten a bit better for the cheaper option.

## What to check before you sign — student flat edition

The general advice in [our rental viewing questions guide](/posts/questions-to-ask-at-a-rental-viewing-nz/) and [signing your first tenancy agreement](/posts/signing-your-first-tenancy-agreement-nz/) covers most of it. A few things matter more for student flats specifically:

**Heating.** A lot of older Auckland student flats fail the [Healthy Homes Standards](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/healthy-homes/) on heating. Since 1 July 2025, every rental must have a fixed heater that can warm the main living area to 18°C. Plug-in panel heaters don't count. Ask to see the Healthy Homes compliance statement at the viewing — if they can't produce it, that's a flag.

**Insulation.** Ceiling and underfloor insulation has been compulsory for rentals since 2019. In converted villas (common around Mt Eden, Sandringham, Grey Lynn, Eden Terrace), check whether the insulation has been retrofitted properly. A genuinely cold flat in winter can cost you $100+/week extra in power.

**Mould and damp.** Look in the corners of bedrooms, behind curtains, around window frames. Smell the wardrobe. South-facing rooms in older flats catch the worst of it.

**Number of people on the lease.** Make sure the agreement names every flatmate who'll be living there. If you sublet a room without the landlord knowing, you're technically in breach — and so is your subletter, with no Tenancy Services protection.

**Length of the lease vs the academic year.** Most fixed-term student leases run 12 months, often starting in late January or February. If you're moving in mid-year, check the end date carefully — you don't want to be locked in until the following March if you'll be back home for summer.

**The bond split.** When four flatmates each pay $600, that's $2,400 in bond. Tenancy Services lets each tenant lodge their share separately or as a group — make sure your name is on the bond receipt, not just the head tenant's. Verify lodgement at [tenancy.govt.nz](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/).

> **Warning:** Application fees, credit-check fees, and "admin fees" are illegal in NZ under the Residential Tenancies Act 1986. Bond (max 4 weeks) and rent in advance (max 2 weeks) are the only upfront payments any landlord can legally require. If you're being charged something else, push back — or walk.

## A realistic timing plan

Auckland's rental market moves fastest in November–February as outgoing students vacate and incoming ones look. If you're starting at UoA in March, the sweet spot for finding a flat is late November through January.

A rough timeline:

- **September–October (year before):** decide whether you're going halls or flatting. Apply for halls now.
- **November–December:** if flatting, start watching listings, ask around in your year-group chats, look for friends-of-friends with rooms opening up.
- **January:** view places. Most good flats let in early-to-mid January. Be ready to apply within hours of viewing.
- **February:** move in, lodge your bond, take photos of every room before unpacking.

If you've left it later than this, don't panic — there's always movement in March and again at mid-year as people drop out, transfer, or move overseas. The pickings get thinner but they don't disappear.

## Flatting with people you don't know

Most students end up in flats with at least one stranger by second or third year. Two practical things help.

**Talk about money before you move in.** How are bills split — evenly, or by income? Who pays the internet bill on the 5th of the month? What happens if someone's late? A 10-minute conversation up front saves a year of low-grade resentment.

**Agree on a flat agreement.** Not a tenancy agreement — a flatmate agreement. Who cleans what, how long-term guests work, what happens if someone wants to move out before the lease ends. [Tenancy Services has a free template](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/starting-a-tenancy/tenancy-agreements/flat-sharing/) that's worth using.

For more on the flatting side specifically, our [student flat guide for Auckland](/posts/student-flat-guide-auckland/) walks through the full lifecycle — finding flatmates, surviving winter, moving out — in more detail.

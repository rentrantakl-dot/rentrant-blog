The University of Auckland has confirmed that **Grafton Student Flats will not be accepting new tenancies from 2026 onwards**. That removes a known supply of affordable, university-managed accommodation from the market — and pushes the students who would have lived there directly into the private rental market in the same area.

Those students — often first-year renters, many of them international students without NZ rental history — will be signing leases in Eden Terrace and Grafton under time pressure, in an area they don't know, with property managers who know they don't know. That information gap is exactly where bad outcomes happen.

This guide is for them, and for anyone else moving into Auckland's apartment belt without a local network to fall back on.

Eden Terrace and Grafton's selling point is straightforward: ten minutes' walk to UoA and AUT, five minutes to the CBD fringe, dense enough that new listings appear regularly. For students and young professionals who need proximity above everything else, this is the trade-off suburb.

The catch is that "apartment in Eden Terrace" covers an enormous range. A newer managed building with on-site maintenance and decent insulation is a very different proposition from a 1990s block with paper-thin walls, no fixed heating, and a body corporate that hasn't spent meaningfully on the building in a decade. Both cost similar amounts to rent. From the listing photos, they can look identical.

Under New Zealand tenancy law, your rights in an apartment are the same as in a standalone house. Your landlord must lodge your bond with Tenancy Services within 23 working days of receiving it and meet the Healthy Homes Standards — **fully in effect since 1 July 2025** — regardless of whether the building is managed by a property manager, owner-operator, or student housing company.

## What You'll Pay

**Overall median rent:** Around **$595/week** across all property types (early 2026) — and rents in the City Centre cluster have been flat or slightly declining year-on-year.

| Property type | Median weekly rent | Year-on-year change |
|---------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| 1-bedroom | $445 | −2% |
| 2-bedroom | $587 | −1% |
| 3-bedroom | $832 | 0% |
| 4-bedroom | $995 | −4% |

*Data: [Crockers / REINZ Auckland Rental Review](https://www.crockers.co.nz/knowledge-hub/auckland-rental-prices/may-2025/), City Centre / Parnell / Grafton / Newton cluster, May 2025.*

Turnover in this area is high — this is useful to know if you need somewhere quickly at the start of semester, but it also means you shouldn't assume you're stuck if the building turns out to be a problem. Use the [Tenancy Services Market Rent Tool](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/rent-bond-and-bills/market-rent/) to check bond lodgement data before you commit to a specific building.

## Getting Around

### Walking

This is the area's main advantage. The University of Auckland's City Campus is roughly 10 minutes on foot. AUT's City Campus is similar. The CBD is a 5–15 minute walk depending on where in Eden Terrace you are. If you're a student or CBD worker who can manage without a car, this area is as close to car-free Auckland living as it gets.

### Bus

Multiple routes connect Eden Terrace and Grafton to the wider city. Grafton Road has regular services and connects through to the eastern suburbs and Newmarket. The inner-city bus network is dense here — the AT Journey Planner will give you accurate current schedules.

### Train

**Grafton station** is on the Southern and Eastern rail lines, with services through to Newmarket, Remuera, and Manukau on one side, and connections into the city on the other. Once the City Rail Link opens in the second half of 2026, Grafton's position in the network improves further — it'll connect through to the new underground CBD stations. If you're in the Grafton end of the area, this is useful to factor in.

### Car

You probably don't need one if you're here for the university proximity. If you do have a car, parking in the area is challenging and often expensive — check whether your building has an included space before signing.

## The Housing Stock

### What's Here

Eden Terrace is primarily **apartment buildings and terraced townhouses** from the 1990s through to the early 2020s. Grafton has a similar mix with some older stock and student-oriented buildings close to the university and Auckland City Hospital.

Most of the supply is apartments rather than freestanding homes. Body corporate-managed buildings are common. That means:

- Shared amenities (laundry, car parks, lifts, lobbies) — quality varies by building
- A landlord who may be an individual apartment owner rather than a full-time property manager
- Building maintenance decisions made by the body corporate, which the landlord doesn't fully control
- Noise — walls and floors between apartments vary significantly in acoustic insulation

### What to Watch For

The era of construction matters a lot here. Buildings from the 1990s and early 2000s — sometimes called "leaky buildings" if they had weathertight issues — should be checked carefully. Not all 1990s apartments are problem buildings, but it's worth asking directly whether the building has had any weathertight remediation or remedial work done.

Under New Zealand's Healthy Homes Standards — **fully in effect since 1 July 2025** — apartment landlords must also comply with heating, ventilation, and moisture requirements. In apartments, the relevant standards often focus on:

- **Heating:** A fixed heating source capable of warming the main living area to 18°C — this applies in apartments just as in houses
- **Ventilation:** Mechanical ventilation in kitchens and bathrooms — key in apartments without natural cross-flow
- **Moisture:** Extractor fans that actually work; no condensation issues caused by inadequate ventilation

> **Warning:** Many apartments fail on heating — a small panel heater plugged into the wall does not satisfy the Healthy Homes heating requirement. Ask for the written compliance statement and check what heating is listed.

Other things to check at a viewing:

- **Noise:** At the viewing, test the walls and floor for sound — knock, listen. Ask what's above and beside you.
- **Natural light:** Lower-floor apartments in tight buildings can be significantly darker than photos suggest
- **Aspect:** North-facing apartments retain warmth; south-facing ones in Auckland can be cold and damp
- **Building age:** Ask when the building was last re-clad or had major maintenance done

See our [Healthy Homes guide](/posts/is-your-auckland-rental-healthy-homes-compliant/) for what to ask and what to do if compliance doesn't check out.

## Applying for a Flat Here — What You Should Know

This area has high application volumes. A listing in Eden Terrace close to the university can receive 20–50+ applications. That creates pressure to submit fast and stand out — which is exactly when renters get asked for things they shouldn't be asked for.

A Consumer NZ mystery shopping study found that **1 in 10 New Zealand property managers encouraged applicants to provide extra personal information** beyond what's legally permissible — including bank statements (6% of agents), race or ethnicity details, employment status, and disability information. Agents told mystery shoppers things like *"the more information you give, the better your chances"* — which is misleading at best, and potentially a Privacy Act violation.

> **Warning:** Property managers cannot legally require you to provide bank statements, photos of yourself, race or ethnicity information, religious beliefs, or employment status as a condition of applying for a rental. If you're asked for these, you're entitled to decline. Providing extra personal information doesn't improve your application — it just exposes you to screening criteria that shouldn't legally exist.

What you *should* expect to provide: proof of income (payslips, a letter from your employer or institution), your rental history and references, and standard ID. That's it.

For international students without NZ rental history: mention your student visa, your institution, and your guarantor (a NZ-based contact willing to be responsible for the tenancy) if you have one. You can also offer to pay a few weeks' rent in advance — within the legal limit of 2 weeks — as a demonstration of reliability, though you're not required to.

## The Vibe

This is where expectation-setting matters. Eden Terrace is not a neighbourhood in the traditional sense. There's no weekend farmers' market, no village strip, no "community" identity to speak of. What it has is:

**What's good:**
- **Location is unmatched** for students and CBD workers — the walk-to-work or walk-to-campus proposition is the whole point
- **Auckland City Hospital** is in Grafton (2 Park Road) — if you work in healthcare, the commute is zero
- **Newmarket** is a short walk south, with better shopping and food options than the immediate area
- High turnover means properties come up frequently — easier to move if a building doesn't work for you
- No car needed — real public transport and walking coverage

**What's less good:**
- The area has no real neighbourhood character — it's a transit zone, not a destination
- Apartment noise and thin walls are common complaints in older buildings
- Green space is limited — Victoria Park (near Ponsonby) and the Domain (near Grafton) are the nearest meaningful reserves, both a 15–20 minute walk
- Some buildings have had weathertight or structural issues — due diligence on specific buildings is more important here than in other suburbs
- Social life happens elsewhere — you'll be heading to Ponsonby, K Road, or the CBD for anything beyond the basics

## Who Suits Eden Terrace and Grafton?

This area works best for:

- **Students at UoA or AUT** who want to walk to campus without the full cost of inner-city
- **Healthcare workers at Auckland City Hospital** — Grafton's proximity is rare and genuinely valuable
- **CBD workers prioritising commute above lifestyle** — everything else can be managed
- **People who don't want or need a car** — the walking and bus access makes car-free living realistic
- **Short-to-medium term renters** who want proximity without a long-term commitment to one neighbourhood

It's less suited to families, people who prioritise outdoor space, or anyone who finds apartment living acoustically challenging.

## Quick Checklist for Renting in Eden Terrace / Grafton

**Before your viewing:**
- [ ] Checked the [Market Rent Tool](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/rent-bond-and-bills/market-rent/) for current Eden Terrace prices
- [ ] Researched the specific building — Google the address + "leaky building" or "remediation"
- [ ] Noted comparable listings to [assess if the rent is fair](/posts/how-to-tell-if-your-rent-is-fair/)

**At the viewing:**
- [ ] Asked for the Healthy Homes compliance statement
- [ ] Tested noise levels — knock on walls, listen for sounds from adjacent units and the street
- [ ] Checked the fixed heating — is there a compliant heater in the main room?
- [ ] Checked extractor fans in kitchen and bathroom — do they work?
- [ ] Checked the aspect — north or south facing? How much natural light in winter?
- [ ] Asked about building maintenance history and body corporate levies (if relevant)

**Before signing:**
- [ ] Confirmed bond is no more than 4 weeks' rent and will be [lodged with Tenancy Services](/posts/your-bond-rights-nz/)
- [ ] Photographed every room and all existing marks before moving in
- [ ] Confirmed parking arrangements if you have a vehicle — do not assume

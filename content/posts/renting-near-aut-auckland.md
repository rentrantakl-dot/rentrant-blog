AUT runs across three campuses, and where you live makes a real difference depending on which one you're studying at. City Campus is in central Auckland, sandwiched against the University of Auckland — same flatting market, same commute options. North Campus is on the Shore. South Campus is in Manukau. The right suburb is the one that minimises the trips you don't want to be making at 8am in July.

Here's what each commute looks like, what halls and private flats actually cost, and what to check before you sign.

---

## Three campuses, three flatting markets

| Campus | Location | Faculties / programmes |
|---|---|---|
| **City Campus** | Wellesley Street East, central Auckland | Business, Communication, Design, Computer & Mathematical Sciences, Law, most undergraduate programmes |
| **North Campus** | 90 Akoranga Drive, Northcote | Health Sciences, Sport & Recreation, Education, Social Sciences |
| **South Campus** | 640 Great South Road, Manukau | Health Sciences (selected programmes), Engineering pathways, Business |

If your timetable is split across two campuses — common in some Health Sciences programmes that run lectures at City Campus and labs at North — pick a flat that gives you a tolerable commute to both. The Northern Busway makes North-to-City a 25–30 minute trip, but doing it five days a week wears thin.

For most students, though, "near AUT" means near the campus where you spend your week.

---

## AUT halls of residence

AUT runs purpose-built student accommodation on both City and North Campuses. The two main City Campus options are the **Wellesley Student Apartments** (just off Wellesley Street, two minutes' walk to lectures) and the **Mayoral Drive Apartments**. North Campus has the **Akoranga Student Village**, walking distance to the campus.

Approximate weekly costs (always confirm against the [AUT Accommodation page](https://www.aut.ac.nz/student-life/accommodation) before committing):

| Residence | Campus | Approx weekly cost (2026) |
|---|---|---|
| Wellesley Student Apartments | City | $400–$520 |
| Mayoral Drive Apartments | City | $360–$470 |
| Akoranga Student Village | North | $370–$470 |

Most are self-catered apartment-style rooms — you'll have your own room and share kitchen and bathroom with two to four flatmates. Power, water, and internet are typically included; food is not. Apply early — AUT halls fill up before the start of each year and there are usually waiting lists by November.

> **Key point:** Hall contracts are usually 38–44 weeks. If you're going home for summer that fits, but if you're staying in Auckland — for a job, summer school, or anything else — you'll need to plan separate accommodation for the gap. Don't assume your room is yours over summer.

## City Campus: where students flat

AUT City and the University of Auckland are essentially next-door neighbours. The same suburbs work for both, and the same advice applies. If you're choosing between them, the question is mostly trade-off between rent and commute time.

|---|---|---|
| Eden Terrace | $280–$340/week | 10–15 min walk |
| Newton | $270–$330/week | 10–15 min walk |
| Grafton | $280–$340/week | 15–20 min walk |
| City Centre apartments | $290–$380/week | 5–10 min walk |
| Kingsland | $260–$320/week | 15 min by bus or train |
| Mt Eden | $270–$330/week | 15–20 min by bus |
| Sandringham | $240–$290/week | 20–25 min by bus |
| Mt Albert | $240–$290/week | 20 min by Western Line train |
| Avondale | $220–$270/week | 25–30 min by Western Line train |
| Henderson | $210–$260/week | 35–40 min by Western Line train |

Room rents are indicative — what you pay depends on the property, number of flatmates, and what's included. For specific suburb benchmarks, the [Tenancy Services Market Rent tool](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/rent-bond-and-bills/market-rent/) shows recent signed rents.

The City Rail Link comes online through 2026, and the Maungawhau (Mt Eden) interchange shortens the trip from Western Line suburbs into the central city. If you're weighing Eden Terrace at $310/week against Mt Albert at $260/week, the train math is moving in favour of the cheaper option.

For more on the City Campus flatting belt — including the same suburbs from the UoA angle — see [renting near the University of Auckland](/posts/renting-near-university-of-auckland/).

## North Campus: the Shore option

If you're at AUT North, the practical play is to live on the Shore. Akoranga Station is on the Northern Busway, and the busway connects most of the North Shore to campus directly.

| Suburb | Approx room rent (shared flat) | Commute to North Campus |
|---|---|---|
| Northcote | $240–$300/week | 10 min walk or one bus stop |
| Birkenhead | $230–$290/week | 10–15 min by bus |
| Takapuna | $260–$330/week | 10–15 min by bus or busway |
| Glenfield | $220–$280/week | 15–20 min by bus |
| Sunnynook | $220–$280/week | Busway, 5 min |
| Constellation | $220–$290/week | Busway, 5–10 min |
| Albany | $250–$310/week | Busway, 10–15 min |
| Devonport | $260–$320/week | Bus 15–20 min, or ferry + bus |

The Northern Busway is genuinely good — express services run every few minutes at peak, and journeys are predictable. Living one or two stops up the busway from Akoranga is often cheaper than living closer in Northcote or Takapuna, with a five-to-ten-minute commute trade.

Living on the Shore and studying City Campus is doable — the busway runs straight through to Britomart in 25–30 minutes — but it's a long week if your timetable is City-heavy.

## South Campus: where the rents are lower

AUT South in Manukau is well-served by the Southern Line and a number of bus routes. The flatting market here is the cheapest of any AUT campus, and rooms are often larger.

| Suburb | Approx room rent (shared flat) | Commute to South Campus |
|---|---|---|
| Manukau | $200–$260/week | 5–10 min walk from Manukau Station |
| Papatoetoe | $200–$250/week | 5–10 min by Southern Line train |
| Ōtāhuhu | $200–$250/week | 10–15 min by Southern Line train |
| Manurewa | $190–$240/week | 5–10 min by Southern Line train |
| Māngere | $190–$240/week | 15–25 min by bus |

If your degree is solely at South Campus, the rent savings are real — a $230/week room here vs a $310/week room near City Campus is roughly $4,000/year. If you ever need to spend a day at City Campus the train is straightforward (45 min from Manurewa to Britomart) but you wouldn't want to do it every day.

## What to check before you sign — AUT student flat edition

Most of the general advice applies — see [our rental viewing questions guide](/posts/questions-to-ask-at-a-rental-viewing-nz/) and [signing your first tenancy agreement in NZ](/posts/signing-your-first-tenancy-agreement-nz/) for the full picture. A few things matter more for AUT student flats:

**The Healthy Homes compliance statement.** Since 1 July 2025, every NZ rental must meet the [Healthy Homes Standards](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/healthy-homes/) — fixed heating, ceiling and underfloor insulation, ventilation, moisture control, and draught stopping. Ask to see the statement at the viewing. If they can't produce it, that's a flag.

**Heating in older Shore villas.** A lot of older Devonport, Northcote, and Birkenhead flats are weatherboard villas with the heating bare-minimum upgraded. The compliance statement will tell you the heater output and the room area — do the math, or ask for it spelled out.

**Aspect and damp.** South-facing rooms in any Auckland flat catch the worst of winter cold and moisture. In tightly packed areas like Eden Terrace and apartment-heavy Manukau, blocked-in flats with no winter sun get cold and stay cold.

**Lease length vs your study load.** Most fixed-term student leases are 12 months, often starting late January or February. If you're starting at AUT mid-year (Semester 2), check the lease end date — you don't want to be locked in for an extra summer if you're not going to be there.

**Bond on your name.** When the bond goes to Tenancy Services it can be lodged jointly or per tenant. Make sure your name is on the receipt — verify lodgement at [tenancy.govt.nz](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/).

> **Warning:** Application fees, credit-check fees, and "admin fees" are illegal under the Residential Tenancies Act 1986. The only legal upfront payments are bond (max 4 weeks' rent) and rent in advance (max 2 weeks). Anything else, push back.

## Timing and the Auckland student rental cycle

Auckland's rental market turns over fastest in November–February as outgoing students move out and incoming ones look. If you're starting at AUT in late February or early March, the realistic window is:

- **September–October:** apply for AUT halls if you're going that route.
- **November–December:** start watching listings if you're flatting; ask in your year-group chats.
- **January:** view, apply, sign. Be ready to commit within hours of viewing — competitive flats don't sit.
- **February:** move in, photograph everything, lodge your bond, verify it.

Mid-year intake (Semester 2, July) has a quieter market with less competition, but also less choice. Six-month leases are rare — you'll often need to take a 12-month commitment.

## Flatting with strangers — two things to sort early

By second year most AUT students are flatting with at least one person they've never met. Two things help:

**Talk about money before you move in.** How are bills split, who's responsible for what, what happens if someone's late on rent. A 10-minute conversation up front saves a year of low-grade resentment.

**Sign a flatmate agreement.** Not a tenancy agreement — that's the legal one with the landlord. A flatmate agreement is between you and the people you live with, covering chores, guests, what happens if someone leaves early. [Tenancy Services has a free template](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/starting-a-tenancy/tenancy-agreements/flat-sharing/).

For more on the flatting lifecycle as an Auckland student — finding flatmates, surviving winter, moving out — see [the student flat guide for Auckland](/posts/student-flat-guide-auckland/).

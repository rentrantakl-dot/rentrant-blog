Getting approved for a rental in Auckland isn't mysterious. Landlords are checking a short list of things: can you afford it, will you pay reliably, will you look after the place. Your application either demonstrates those three things clearly — or it doesn't.

---

## What They're Actually Checking

| What landlords assess | How they check it |
|---|---|
| Can this person afford the rent? | Payslips, employment letter, bank statements |
| Will they pay reliably? | Rental references and employment stability |
| Will they look after the property? | References and how the application is presented |
| How many people will live there? | Application form |
| Are there pets? | Application form |

---

## What to Have Ready

- Government-issued photo ID
- Proof of income — most recent 2 payslips, employment letter on letterhead, or student loan confirmation
- Contact details for a previous landlord (with their permission to be called)
- A clear statement of who will live in the property

**One thing most applicants skip:** Call your reference before you apply. Tell them to expect a call and give them the context. A reference who answers immediately and gives a specific account is worth more than one who takes four days to respond.

---

## What Not to Do

- Don't offer more rent than listed — it raises questions rather than improving your application
- Don't omit pets and plan to bring them in — it's a lease breach and damages trust when discovered
- Don't pad references with friends who can't speak to your tenancy
- Don't apply for places where the numbers don't work — the income check is first

---

## Illegal Fees

Bond (maximum 4 weeks) and rent in advance (maximum 2 weeks) are the only upfront payments required. Application fees, admin fees, and letting fees charged to tenants are illegal. If you're asked for anything else, question it.

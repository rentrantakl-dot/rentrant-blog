The bond stays with [Tenancy Services](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/) until the whole tenancy ends. Your departing flatmate sorts their share with the household, not with Tenancy Services — and a Change of Tenant form keeps the paperwork tidy.

This trips people up every flatting season. Here's how it actually works in New Zealand.

---

## The Bond Belongs to the Tenancy, Not to You

Under the [Residential Tenancies Act 1986](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1986/0120/latest/whole.html), bond is lodged against the tenancy — not against individual tenants. Tenancy Services holds it in a single pot until the tenancy ends, then refunds it once everyone agrees on the split (or the Tribunal decides).

So when one flatmate leaves mid-tenancy: Tenancy Services won't refund their portion. There's nothing to split until the end.

---

## How a Flatmate Swap Actually Works

The standard process when one flatmate goes and another comes in:

1. **Find the replacement.** The household and the landlord both have to agree to them.
2. **The incoming flatmate pays the leaving one** directly for their bond share — typically by bank transfer.
3. **Lodge a [Change of Tenant form](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/starting-a-tenancy/types-of-tenancies/changing-tenants/)** on tenancy.govt.nz. Everyone signs: outgoing tenant, incoming tenant, remaining tenants, landlord.
4. **Tenancy Services updates the bond record.** The bond doesn't move — only the names on it do.

That's it. The new flatmate is now formally on the tenancy and on the bond.

---

## Head-Tenant Setups Are Different

If only one person is named on the tenancy agreement and the rest of you are flatmates under them, you're not covered by the RTA in the same way. The head tenant is the only one Tenancy Services deals with — they carry the bond liability and they decide who comes and goes.

In that setup, your "bond" is really money paid to the head tenant. Getting it back when you leave is between you and them, not Tenancy Services. A written flatmate agreement (and a paper trail showing what you paid) is the only thing that protects you here.

For the full picture on flatmate vs tenant rights, see [Flatting in Auckland: Your Rights as a Flatmate](/posts/flatting-in-auckland-your-rights-as-a-flatmate/).

---

## If the Replacement Won't Pay Up

If the incoming flatmate refuses to pay the outgoing one for the bond share, it's a civil debt — not a Tenancy Tribunal matter (the Tribunal only handles disputes between landlords and tenants, not flatmate-to-flatmate). Your option is the [Disputes Tribunal](https://www.disputestribunal.govt.nz/), which handles claims under $30,000 without a lawyer.

> **Key point:** Don't move out until the Change of Tenant form is signed and the bond share is in your account. Once you're off the tenancy without paperwork, getting your share back is much harder.

You've got 15 minutes in someone else's flat. The agent is friendly. The curtains are open. And you need to figure out if this place is worth applying for.

Your eyes will catch some things. Your nose will catch others. But the questions you ask — and how they're answered — will tell you the rest.

---

## The Five Questions

**1. "Can I see the Healthy Homes compliance statement?"**

Landlords must provide this before the tenancy starts. Asking at the viewing tests whether they have it ready. Good answer: they produce it or confirm it comes with the agreement. Red flag: confusion, "we're working on it," or a blank look.

**2. "Does the kitchen range hood vent outside, or is it recirculating?"**

Recirculating range hoods are explicitly non-compliant under the Healthy Homes ventilation standard (in force since 1 July 2025). This is a specific compliance question almost no viewer asks. Good answer: "vents outside — there's the exhaust vent." Red flag: "it has a charcoal filter" means it recirculates.

**3. "Why did the previous tenants leave?"**

Good answer: end of fixed term, moved overseas, bought a place. Red flag: evasion, vague "personal reasons," a subject change. Cross-check against how long the listing has been up.

**4. "Who handles maintenance? What's the typical response time for urgent repairs?"**

This is the most predictive question you can ask — the maintenance relationship is where most tenancies succeed or fail. Good answer: named person, specific timeframe, clear escalation path. Red flag: "the owner is really responsive" without any specifics.

**5. "Is there anything about the property not in the listing I should know?"**

Agents have obligations to disclose known material facts. This question invites those disclosures. You won't always get an answer — but the absence of one is also data.

---

## The Meta-Signal

Pay attention to the pattern of how they answer, not just what they say. A property manager who knows their building answers immediately and specifically, without reaching for their phone. The person you're talking to at the viewing is probably the person taking your maintenance call at 7pm in June.

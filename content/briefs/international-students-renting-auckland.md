You're finding a flat in a city you've never been to, from a country you're still in, with a course start date closing in. That pressure is exactly what Auckland rental scammers rely on. Here's what you need before you sign anything.

---

## The Scam Warning — Read This First

If someone on WeChat, RedNote, or Facebook asks for money before you have a signed agreement and physical keys in hand — stop. It's a scam.

NZ Police have specifically warned about fake listings targeting Chinese-background students via WeChat. The pattern: plausible listing, fake documents, urgent request to transfer a deposit. The money disappears. The flat doesn't exist.

Stick to NZ-based platforms: Trade Me, realestate.co.nz, Flatmates.co.nz. University accommodation is the safest starting point.

---

## How the Money Works

| Item | Maximum allowed (at $500/wk) |
|------|------------------------------|
| Bond | 4 weeks = $2,000 |
| Rent in advance | 2 weeks = $1,000 |
| Application / admin fees | **Illegal — $0** |

Bond is **not held by your landlord** — by law it must be lodged with Tenancy Services (a government body) within 23 working days. You can verify it at tenancy.govt.nz. If it hasn't been lodged, your landlord is in breach of the Act.

---

## No NZ Rental History? Here's What Helps

- Bank statements or a sponsor letter showing you can cover rent
- A NZ-based guarantor (family member or trusted contact willing to co-sign)
- University halls for year one — gives you a NZ reference for private rentals after
- Offer 2 weeks' rent in advance (legal max — never offer more, even if pressured)

---

## Key Rights

- **Rent increases:** once every 12 months, 60 days' written notice required
- **Inspections:** 48 hours' notice, between 8am–7pm, maximum once every 4 weeks
- **Ending a periodic tenancy:** you give 21 days' notice; landlord must give 90 days
- **Healthy Homes Standards** apply to your flat — heating, insulation, ventilation are legally required
- **Disputes:** Tenancy Tribunal, ~$20 to file, no lawyer needed. Free help: Community Law NZ or Citizens Advice Bureau

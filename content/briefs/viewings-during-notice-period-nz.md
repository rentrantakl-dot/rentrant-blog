Yes — but only with your consent. Even once either party has served notice to end the tenancy, the flat's still your home. You can't unreasonably say no to viewings, but they happen on terms you agree — not on the landlord's schedule.

---

## What the Law Says

Under [section 48(3) of the Residential Tenancies Act 1986](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1986/0120/latest/DLM95504.html), a landlord may enter to show the property to prospective tenants or buyers **only with your prior consent**. There's no notice period that substitutes for it. The balance sits in section 48(3A): you may not withhold consent unreasonably, but you may impose **reasonable conditions**.

That means:

| Request | Your position |
|---|---|
| "Can we view Thursday at 5pm?" asked in advance | Say yes, or propose a time that works — refusing everything isn't reasonable |
| "I'll be showing the flat every Saturday morning for the next month" | You don't have to accept a blanket arrangement — agree specific visits or set a weekly cap |
| Same-day message asking to pop in | You can say no — consent is yours to give |
| Turning up unannounced with prospective tenants | Unlawful entry under section 48 |

One yes isn't blanket consent for all future viewings. Agree the arrangement — days, times, how often — and put it in writing.

---

## During a Notice Period

It doesn't matter who served notice first. Until the tenancy actually ends, the entry rules are the same. Your landlord can start showing the flat once notice is in — but only on the consent terms you've agreed, at reasonable times.

If viewings start happening daily, or seem timed to make you uncomfortable rather than to find a new tenant, that's worth noting. Under [section 38 of the RTA](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1986/0120/latest/DLM95082.html), you have a right to quiet enjoyment. Excessive viewings used as pressure tactics can be challenged at the Tenancy Tribunal.

---

## What to Do If Your Landlord Ignores the Rules

Write to them (text or email is fine) and name the issue: "You entered on [date] to show the property without my consent." Keep a record. If it continues, [apply to the Tenancy Tribunal](https://www.justice.govt.nz/tribunals/tenancy-tribunal/) — the fee is $29, and unlawful entry is a straightforward claim.

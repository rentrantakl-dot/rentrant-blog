Grafton sits between two universities, Auckland City Hospital, and a motorway. The University of Auckland's Grafton Student Flats are no longer accepting new tenancies from 2026 — those students are now in the private market, competing for the same flats as everyone else.

---

## What You'll Pay

| Bedrooms | Median weekly rent |
|----------|-------------------|
| 1-bed | ~$445/week |
| 2-bed | ~$587/week |
| 3-bed | ~$832/week |

Rents have been flat to slightly declining — there's some room to negotiate, especially at the upper end.

---

## Why People Rent Here

- **University of Auckland & AUT** — roughly 10 minutes on foot
- **Auckland City Hospital** (2 Park Road) — walking distance for healthcare workers
- **Grafton Train Station** — Southern and Eastern lines; CRL connection coming in 2026

If one of these is your reason for being here, the location justifies the price. Otherwise, there are cheaper suburbs with better character.

---

## What to Watch For

Most of the stock is apartments — 1990s through 2010s builds, often body corporate managed.

- **Weathertight risk** — if the building is pre-2004, ask directly whether it's had remediation work
- **Heating** — plug-in panel heaters don't meet Healthy Homes standards; there must be a fixed heater capable of 18°C in the main room
- **Aspect** — south-facing apartments in tight blocks can be cold and damp

---

## Applying

High application volumes. You only need to provide: proof of income, rental history, and ID.

Property managers **cannot** legally ask for bank statements, photos, or information about your ethnicity, religion, or employment status. You're entitled to decline if they do.

International students: include your visa details, institution, and a NZ-based guarantor if you have one.

## Before You Sign

- [ ] Check recent signed rents at [tenancy.govt.nz/rent-bond-and-bills/market-rent](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/rent-bond-and-bills/market-rent/)
- [ ] Ask about weathertight history if the building is pre-2004
- [ ] Confirm a compliant fixed heater is in the main living area
- [ ] Test extractor fans in kitchen and bathroom
- [ ] Knock on walls at the viewing — noise is a common complaint in older blocks
- [ ] Bond: max 4 weeks' rent, must be lodged with Tenancy Services

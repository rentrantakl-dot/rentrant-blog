A landlord can ask for bank statements. You don't automatically have to hand over a full unredacted version. Under the [Privacy Act 2020](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/2020/0031/latest/whole.html), landlords can only collect personal information that is **reasonably necessary** to assess your application — and a full statement reveals far more than that.

---

## What They're Actually Trying to Verify

There are usually two things a landlord wants to know from a bank statement:

- **You earn what you say you earn**
- **You're not visibly broke or in financial crisis**

Both of those can be answered without showing every transaction in your account. A recent payslip, an employer letter, or a bank statement with non-income items redacted will prove your income just as well — and that's what the Privacy Act considers reasonable.

---

## What You Can Redact

You're allowed to black out anything that doesn't go to the assessment they've asked you to support. That includes:

- Transactions unrelated to income (rent paid elsewhere, groceries, transfers, subscriptions)
- Account balances at points unrelated to income deposit dates
- Card numbers and account numbers (always)
- Personal details about who you pay or who pays you

What to leave visible: your name, the bank name, and the income deposits with their dates and amounts. That's the bit they need.

---

## What Landlords Cannot Reasonably Ask For

The [Office of the Privacy Commissioner](https://www.privacy.org.nz/) has consistently said landlords must justify each piece of personal information they collect. A few things have been called out as overreach in past advisory work:

| Request | Reasonable? |
|---|---|
| Payslip or employer letter | Yes |
| Bank statement showing only income deposits | Yes |
| Full unredacted bank statements | Hard to justify when alternatives exist |
| Photo of you | No — explicitly flagged as not necessary |
| Application or admin fee | Illegal under the [Residential Tenancies Act 1986](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1986/0120/latest/whole.html) |
| Credit check fee charged to you | Illegal |

---

## What to Do If There's Pushback

Most property managers will accept a redacted statement or a payslip without argument. If one refuses, you have options:

1. **Ask them to explain** — in writing — why a redacted statement isn't enough. The answer often clarifies things one way or the other
2. **Complain to the Privacy Commissioner** — free, via [privacy.org.nz](https://www.privacy.org.nz/)
3. **If the refusal looks discriminatory** — based on ethnicity, family status, employment status — that's a Human Rights Act complaint to [Te Kāhui Tika Tangata](https://tikatangata.org.nz/)

> **Key point:** "Everyone has to provide them" is not a legal justification. The Privacy Act applies per applicant — what's necessary for your application is what the law tests against, not what the agency normally collects.

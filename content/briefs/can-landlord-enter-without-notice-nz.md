No. Under section 48 of the Residential Tenancies Act 1986, your landlord must give you **at least 24 hours written notice** before entering your rental. There is one exception: a genuine emergency.

---

## What the Law Requires

The notice must be in writing and must specify:

- The **date** of entry
- The **time** (must be between 8am and 7pm)
- The **reason** for entry

Email and text message both count as written notice. A verbal heads-up the night before does not.

There is no limit on how often a landlord can inspect — but they must give proper notice every single time. Landlords who inspect frequently without cause can face a Tribunal order limiting inspections.

---

## The Emergency Exception

A landlord can enter without notice only if there is a **genuine emergency** — fire, flood, serious water leak, or similar. The emergency has to be real and immediate. "I was in the area" or "I wanted to check the hot water cylinder" are not emergencies.

If the tenant agrees to entry at that moment — not in advance, but at the time — entry is also permitted. Agreeing in the tenancy agreement that the landlord can enter on short notice generally does not override the statutory requirement.

---

## What Counts as Unlawful Entry

Entering without notice, outside the permitted hours, or without a valid reason is an **unlawful act** under the Residential Tenancies Act. A landlord who does this can face:

- An order from the Tenancy Tribunal to stop
- Exemplary damages — up to $7,200 for individual landlords, up to $50,000 for larger landlords with six or more properties

---

## What to Do

If your landlord enters without proper notice:

1. **Write to them immediately** — email is fine — citing section 48 of the Residential Tenancies Act and asking them to confirm they will give proper notice in future
2. **Keep a record** — dates, times, and what happened
3. **Apply to the Tenancy Tribunal** if it continues — you do not need a lawyer

The [Tenancy Services website](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/) has free information and the application form.

Once both you and your landlord agree on how the bond should be split, [Tenancy Services](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/rent-bond-and-bills/bond/refunding-a-bond/) aims to process the refund within **5–10 working days**. The money lands in your nominated bank account — your landlord never touches it.

---

## Who Actually Holds Your Bond

Your landlord doesn't. Under the [Residential Tenancies Act 1986](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1986/0120/latest/whole.html), every bond must be lodged with Tenancy Services within 23 working days of receipt. It sits in a government trust account until the tenancy ends — so "I'm still waiting on the landlord" is the wrong mental model. Tenancy Services pays you out.

---

## The Normal Timeline

When the tenancy ends and both parties agree on the split (full refund, partial deduction, or whatever you've negotiated):

1. One party fills in the bond refund form online
2. The other party confirms
3. Tenancy Services processes the payment within **5–10 working days**

Most refunds with no dispute clear inside two weeks. You don't need to wait for your landlord to start the process — you can apply first.

---

## When Your Landlord Goes Quiet

If your landlord won't sign the form, won't reply, or is dragging it out, **apply for the refund yourself**. Lodge the application through [Tenancy Services online](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/rent-bond-and-bills/bond/refunding-a-bond/).

What happens next:

- Tenancy Services notifies your landlord
- The landlord has **10 working days** to object
- If they don't respond, Tenancy Services can release the bond to you without their signature

This is the part most tenants don't realise. A silent landlord is not a stuck bond — it's a 10-day clock.

---

## If There's a Genuine Dispute

If your landlord objects and you can't agree, the process moves to mediation through Tenancy Services. Mediation is free and often resolves things in a phone call. If mediation fails, either party can apply to the [Tenancy Tribunal](https://www.justice.govt.nz/tribunals/tenancy-tribunal/) — the application fee is **$28** and you don't need a lawyer.

Tribunal cases take longer (typically two to four months for a hearing), but the Tribunal can order the bond paid out however the evidence supports. If your landlord can't prove damage beyond fair wear and tear, the bond comes back to you.

> **Key point:** Don't sign a bond refund form you don't agree with. Once you sign, the deduction is final. If the split feels wrong, leave the form unsigned and apply for the refund yourself.

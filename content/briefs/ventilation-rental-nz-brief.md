Your bathroom has a window. Your landlord says that's enough. Under New Zealand's Healthy Homes ventilation standard — in force since **1 July 2025** — it isn't.

---

## The Window Rule (Quick Version)

Every bedroom and living area must have openable windows, doors, or skylights totalling at least **5% of the floor area**. That's the baseline. But for kitchens and bathrooms, windows alone aren't enough.

---

## Kitchens: Why Your Range Hood Might Not Count

| Fan type | Compliant? |
|---|---|
| Range hood or fan vented to outside | **Yes** |
| Recirculating range hood (filters and returns air) | **No** |
| HRV or DVS whole-house ventilation system | **No** |
| No fan at all | No |

Tenancy Services names recirculating systems, HRV, and DVS explicitly as non-compliant. If the fan doesn't push air out through a duct to outside, it doesn't meet the standard — regardless of how powerful it sounds when you turn it on.

---

## Bathrooms: A Window Isn't Enough

Bathrooms require an extractor fan vented to the **outdoors**. Not to the roof cavity. Not a window. A fan with a duct that actually exits the building.

Fans installed before 1 July 2019 are grandfathered — but only if they still vent outside and are in working order.

---

## What to Do If Yours Doesn't Comply

1. Put it in writing — email your landlord citing the Healthy Homes ventilation standard (fully in effect since 1 July 2025)
2. If they don't act, apply to the Tenancy Tribunal ($28 to file)
3. The Tribunal can order installation, reduce your rent for the non-compliant period, and award exemplary damages

---

## Quick Checklist

- [ ] Bedrooms and living areas have openable windows that open properly
- [ ] Kitchen has a fan or range hood with a duct to outside (not recirculating)
- [ ] Bathroom has an extractor fan vented outside (not just a window)

Yes — rent is payable throughout the notice period. Whether you gave the notice or your landlord did, rent keeps accruing every day until the tenancy legally ends. There's no provision in the [Residential Tenancies Act 1986](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/rent-bond-and-bills/rent/) that suspends rent when notice is served.

---

## Leaving Before the Notice Period Ends

This is the one that catches people out. Say you give 21 days notice but find a new flat faster than expected and move out on day 10. You're still liable for rent until day 21 — unless your landlord re-lets the property in the meantime.

If the landlord finds a new tenant and they move in before your notice period expires, your rent liability ends from the day the new tenancy starts. You'd only owe rent for the days you were technically still the tenant.

Landlords are required under the Act to take reasonable steps to mitigate their loss — they can't deliberately leave the property empty just to keep billing you. But "reasonable steps" doesn't mean accepting the first applicant at a lower rent. It means genuinely trying.

---

## When the Landlord Gives Notice

Same deal from the other direction. If you receive a 90-day no-cause notice, a 42-day sale notice, or any other landlord termination, rent is due every day until that notice date — even if you find somewhere new in week two.

You can negotiate with the landlord to bring the end date forward. If you both agree and put it in writing, the rent liability ends on the agreed date. Get it signed. A verbal agreement won't hold up at the Tribunal.

---

## Rent in Arrears at Move-Out

If rent is unpaid when you leave, your landlord can apply to the [Tenancy Tribunal](https://www.justice.govt.nz/tribunals/tenancy-tribunal/) for the outstanding amount. Unpaid rent is one of the most common reasons bond deductions are disputed — and if the arrears exceed the bond, the Tribunal can order you to pay the difference.

Keep track of what's paid, when, and keep the receipts or bank records. It removes any ambiguity.

For more on how bond refunds work at the end of tenancy, see [Your Bond Rights in NZ](/posts/your-bond-rights-nz/).

No — your landlord doesn't have to provide curtains in NZ. There's nothing in the Residential Tenancies Act or the Healthy Homes standards that requires it. But if curtains were there when you moved in and they're listed in the tenancy agreement, the landlord has to keep them in working order.

---

## What the Law Actually Requires

The [Healthy Homes standards](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/healthy-homes/) cover heating, insulation, ventilation, moisture and drainage, and draught-stopping. Window coverings aren't on that list. The draught-stopping standard requires unwanted gaps and holes in the building envelope to be closed — but a bare window isn't a "gap" under the standard.

So a landlord can legally rent out a property with no curtains, blinds, or shades. It's not great practice, especially in winter, but it's not unlawful.

---

## When the Landlord Does Have to Maintain Curtains

If curtains were in the property at the start of your tenancy and are listed as fixtures in the [tenancy agreement](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/starting-a-tenancy/tenancy-agreements/) or [property condition report](https://www.tenancy.govt.nz/starting-a-tenancy/inspection/), they become the landlord's responsibility to maintain.

That means:

- If they fall apart through fair wear and tear — landlord replaces them
- If the rail breaks or a runner snaps — landlord fixes it
- If you damage them deliberately or through carelessness — that comes off your bond

Section 45 of the [Residential Tenancies Act 1986](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1986/0120/latest/whole.html) requires landlords to keep the premises in a reasonable state of repair. Once curtains are part of the premises, that duty applies to them.

---

## Installing Your Own

You're allowed to put up your own curtains or blinds. Two things to remember:

1. **Ask first** if you need to drill into walls or window frames. Since the [Residential Tenancies Amendment Act 2020](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/2020/0028/latest/whole.html), landlords can only refuse a minor change on prescribed grounds — but the request still needs to be in writing.
2. **Make good** at the end of the tenancy. Either leave the curtains in place (if the landlord wants them) or remove them and patch the holes.

Tension rods and stick-on blinds avoid the drilling question entirely.
